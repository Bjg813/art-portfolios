About me

Farida Alami is an Award Winning Videographer and Editor who was born in Casablanca, Morocco. Farida moved to Los Angeles, 5 years ago to pursue a career in the entertainment industry.

Farida is an avid surfer. While she was surfing one day, she witnessed the abundance of trash in the ocean. That experience inspired her to produce and direct a documentary called Plastic Disaster.

This documentary has raised awareness on the severity of plastic pollution in our oceans. In addition, Plastic Disaster has won major awards and accolades of critics.

Farida has directed and edited multiple other projects including narratives, promotionals, and music videos.

In her work, she has developed leadership, communication, cinematography, and editing skills. Farida is well versed in Adobe Premiere Pro, After Effects, Photoshop, and Final Cut Pro.

She is an expert with DSLR and professional cameras. Additionally, her skills in drone shots can be seen in Plastic Disaster.

Farida has significant professional experience working for video studios and TV stations. She has been trusted with producing and editing professional work on tight deadlines.

When she is not working on shoots, she spends her time camping with friends. She is fluent in English, French, and Arabic.
